Abstract 
This literature review explores the existing research and systems related to internship and training 
management platforms. The emphasis has been on how digital solutions can enable students to 
discover verified opportunities, prepare for those opportunities, and connect effectively with 
employers. Key studies on Work Integrated Learning (WIL), online assessment, and internshi p 
portals have been highlighted; these indicate that such centralized platforms make internship 
processes more organized, transparent, and accessible. However, most of these existing systems 
lack verification features, progress tracking, and learning suppo rt for students. Based on both the 
review of academic research and the existing applications, the identified gaps are used to guide the 
design of InternQuest, a secure, user -friendly, educationally supportive web platform developed 
to bridge the gap between students and verified recruiters in Nepal.

1. Introduction 
Internships are an important part of a student’s learning journey as they help connect 
classroom knowledge with real-world experience. However, finding reliable and verified 
internship opportunities in Nepal is often a challenge for a student. The different portals 
available are either outdated, unorganized, or lack verification and tracking mechanisms, 
making it difficult for students to trust the validity and manage their job applications. 
With the rapidly increasing reliance on digital platforms, web -based systems will provide 
easy access to and management of internships in a much more transparent way. Research 
into Work-Integrated Learning and online internship systems identifies the need to link 
opportunity management with skills development, monitoring, and feedback tools. These 
findings identify a need for a platform like InternQuest, which will provide connections 
between students and companies but also support learning and preparation through 
embedded resources and assessments. 
It looks at previous studies, systems, and online internship platforms to understand what 
has been done in this area and what gaps still exist. These findings are helpful in shaping 
InternQuest, a platform designed to make internships more accessible, veri fied, and 
educationally supportive for students and employers in Nepal.

2. Literature Review 
2.1 Enhancing Internship Experiences: A Comprehensive Study of 
Internship Portals in Educational Institutions 
Studies have shown that students who participate in internships sourced through portals 
demonstrate higher levels of job readiness and are more competitive in the job market. This 
paper researched the use of internship portals in educational institutions f or improvement in 
the overall internship process. The paper describes different existing portals, comparing their 
features, benefits, and challenges. The internship portals work as digital bridges between 
students and employers, facilitating the posting of  jobs, applications, approvals, and tracking 
of performance on one platform. 
The study underlines that such systems make internships more accessible and inclusive, thus 
giving students from different backgrounds the possibility to explore more opportunities. It 
also mentions some problems, such as unequal access for students, a lac k of quality control, 
and low transparency regarding how the internships are handled. The authors indicate that 
future systems should be accompanied by an improved user experience, data analytics, and 
intelligent matching tools that will eventually link students to suitable employers. (Shriwas, et 
al., 2024) 
2.2 Using Internship Management System to Improve the Relationship 
between Internship Seekers, Employers, and Educational Institutions  
 
The authors examine the possibilities offered by a centralized Internship Management System 
(IMS) to enhance communication and coordination among students, employers, and 
universities. They are interested in the use of digital tools to decrease the manual work and 
make the management of internships more efficient. Their study relies on survey information 
of businesses in Kosovo, North Macedonia, and Albania on the need, design requirements, and 
technical structures of such a system. 
 
One of the outstanding results of this research is the high request on a centralized management 
of internship programs as 87 percent of companies stated that they were in support of using a 
web-based IMS tools. The authors suggest to use MVC (Model-View-Controller) architecture 
and open-source web technologies such as PHP, MySQL and Bootstrap to create scalable and 
maintainable systems that can help the three stakeholders engage in constant interaction. The 
role of clear system design and structure in enha ncing collaboration between all the three 
stakeholders: students, academic mentors, and employers is also highlighted in the paper.  
(Mydyti & Kadriu, 2020)

2.3 Enhancing Employability Through Work Integrated Learning  
This article  explores how Work-Integrated Learning (WIL) , including internships, helps 
prepare graduates for real -world employment. Their study looks at how universities and 
industries can work together to give students meaningful learning experiences that build both 
technical and soft skills. They argue that internships play a major role in connecting what 
students learn in classrooms to what employers expect in the workplace. 
The paper identifies several challenges, such as weak coordination between universities and 
employers, unequal access to quality placements, and poor systems for tracking student 
outcomes. Despite these issues, the research shows that well -structured internship programs 
can improve students’ confidence, employability, and readiness for the job market. The authors 
also suggest that digital internship platforms can help overcome many of these challenges by 
improving coordination, communication, and feedback b etween students, educators, and 
employers. (Kenaphoom & Niyomves, 2023) 
2.4 An Evaluation of Online Proctoring Tools 
A study was carried out at the University of the South Pacific to evaluate popular online 
proctoring systems that became widely used during the COVID -19 pandemic. The research 
examined several tools, including ProctorU, Respondus, and Proctorio, using a fo ur-step 
process that involved background research, expert reviews, test trials, and feedback from both 
students and teachers. 
Results showed that online proctoring, while effective in maintaining the honesty of exams 
through its features such as ID checks, webcam monitoring, browser lockdown, and fraud 
detection, also raises some very critical issues regarding privacy, accessibil ity, cost, and 
comfort. Many students felt uncomfortable with being constantly video monitored , some of 
them also reported technical issues during setup and authentication. The authors concluded 
from these findings that although online proctoring has a role in protecting academic integrity, 
it is not a one -size-fits-all solution. They recommend that institutions use it carefully, 
considering factors like infrastructure, ethics, and inclusivity before implementing such 
systems. (Hussein, et al., 2020)

2.5 How does quiz activity affect summative assessment outcomes?  
This paper examined the influence of formative quizzes on students' final examination scores. 
The study involved 217 first-year medical students over three years, required students to attend 
a medical informatics course. Students had an option to voluntarily undertake quizzes as self-
directed learning ( SDL) using the Moodle platform. The results indicated that students who 
took quizzes performed better in the final exams compared to those who did not take them. 
While students who repeatedly took the quizzes  scored slightly higher than one -timers, the 
difference was too small to reach a level of statistical significance. 
Results also indicated that no strong correlation was found between quiz scores and final exam 
marks, suggesting that the most important benefit of quizzes came from the process of learning 
itself rather than from the scores obtained. The author further ad ded that formative quizzes 
motivate students to study continuously, allow them to identify their learning gaps, and take 
more responsibility towards their learning. The study corroborates earlier literature on how 
repeated testing, along with formative ass essment, aids knowledge retention, motivation, and 
overall academic achievement. Overall, the results indicated that quizzes are a useful tool in 
fostering self-directed and effective learning in education. (Oh & Hyunyong, 2022) 
2.6 Assessing Education Students’ Self-directed Learning Experiences for 
Optimal Online Learning 
A survey was conducted with 154 education students at Caraga State University to study self-
directed learning in online environments. The research looked at students’ motivation, learning 
styles, and readiness to use technology. The findings showed that most students were highly 
motivated and good at managing their learning, setting goals, and completing tasks even with 
distractions. 
However, the same study also revealed that students liked materials that were audio-visual and 
multimodal, said they felt less motivated when teachers were not actively present, and 
sometimes felt anxious or confused when confronted with less familiar digi tal tools. While 
most of the students were comfortable users of technology and saw the internet as an important 
resource, some had difficulty evaluating information found online and using particular devices. 
On the basis of these findings, the authors iden tify three priorities in the design of online 
learning materials: include multimodal content like audio, visuals, and small chunks of text, 
matching learning preferences and internet limits; include frequent, low -stakes quizzes and 
rapid feedback by instructors or automated systems that keep students motivated; and include 
simple guides and support materials to minimize technology -related stress and ensure equity 
of access. (Beray, et al., 14)

2.7 A Review on Factors Affecting the Success of Online Proctoring System 
This paper reviews the recent research on online proctoring systems and discuss the factors 
that determine the success of the systems in actual educational contexts. They find that online 
proctoring may be useful to preserve academic integrity, but its success involves a lot of stable 
technology, effective communication and readiness to use it. Lots of students and teachers 
indicated that they felt privacy was at risk, that they were worrying because they were being 
watched all the time and that there were technical problems like untrusty internet connection 
or malfunctioning software. Another issue that the authors emphasize is the disparity in digital 
access and familiarity with technology that may cause unequal experience to students, and 
make the system seem unfair to some students. 
The paper also highlights the fact that effective online proctoring does not only rely on the 
tools, but the institutions also have to promote the use of the tools. Instruction, practice, and 
technical support of students can alleviate the stress of the st udents and will enhance their 
acceptance. The authors however indicate that most of the available literature have small 
sample sizes or are based in one institution and it is hard to generalize findings. They suggest 
greater assessment systems and more focus on ethical and equity concerns. (Al Najar & Ahmad, 
2024) 
 
2.8 Defining Common Elements of Quality Work-Integrated Learning with a   
Literature Review 
A literature review has been performed in this paper to determine the fundamental aspects that 
render the Work-Integrated Learning (WIL) experiences effective and quality to students. This 
paper integrates the results of different models of WIL, including internships, placements and 
project-based learning, to establish what good industry education partnerships ought to be. The 
review identifies a number of shared features of quality WIL such as articulated learning 
outcomes, meaningful workplace activities,  guided supervision, reflective opportunities as 
well as effective communication between institutions and employers. The points that this paper 
stresses are that WIL is only effective when students have expectations and feedback 
frequently and when they ar e in a setting that actually favors learning as opposed to mere 
sources of labor.  
Another observation in the paper is that even with the increased application of WIL, most of 
the programs are faced with inconsistent supervision, ambiguous methods of assessment and 
lack of preparation of both the students and the host organizations. Fent on states that the 
institutions should have standardised guidelines and more supportive structures so that the 
placements are fair and even have similar quality. (Fenton, 2025)

2.9 Self-directed learning: assessment of students’ abilities and their 
perspective 
The paper discusses self -directed learning (SDL) skills of students in the setting of Indian 
transition to competency-based medical education. The study conducted a study using a validated 
questionnaire (SDLI) among 74 students and discovered that learners  were generally highly 
motivated, aware of their strengths and weaknesses, and were willing to improve and take 
responsibility of their learning. Students said that they like to discover answers by themselves and 
can tend to set goals, track progress, and assess results. Nevertheless, the outcomes also showed 
some weak points with time management, the skill of oral communication, and the inability to find 
the appropriate learning materials being the most prominent ones.  
The qualitative data revealed that students tend to think that SDL is most effective when they are 
concentrated, motivated, and encouraged in an appropriate learning environment. They further 
stressed on the need to conduct frequent interactive sessions like group discussions, presentations, 
quizzes, and peer teaching to make them active and interested. In general, the analysis indicates 
that, although the students feel positive about SDL, they require a systematic support, proper 
direction, and the development of skills, particularly in communication and selection of resources, 
so that they can be successful in their role of being independent learners. (Bhandari, et al., 2020) 
 
2.10 A Systematic-Narrative Review of Online Proctoring Systems and a Case 
for Open Standards 
The paper examines the role of online practice test in student learning in virtual courses. Through 
analysis of the student use of practice quizzes, student perception and the results of their 
assessment, the authors discovered that frequent use of practice tests is beneficial in assisting the 
student to comprehend course material better and to be more prepared to take the high stakes test. 
Students noted that such quizzes helped them to know more about the expectations and to point 
out the important topics and lowered the anxiety levels by enabling them to familiarize with the 
format of the exam. 
The results also indicate that students who habitually filled in practice quizzes were more likely to 
achieve better results in their end tests and as such, there exists a positive correlation between 
repeated testing and better academic performance. Nevertheless, the authors underline that the 
success of such quizzes is predetermined by their alignment with learning goals and the feedback 
opportunities, which the students can receive. (Heinrich, 2025)

3. Literature Review Gap Analysis 
Enhancing Internship Experiences article provides a broad understanding of how internship portals 
operate and their role in improving the overall internship process. However, their study focuses 
more on describing existing systems rather than improving them. It does not clearly show how 
these portals can be made more user -friendly, data -driven, or personalized for students and 
employers. Using Internship Management System to Improve the Relationship between Internship 
Seekers, Employers, and Educational Institutions paper contributes by emphasizing the importance 
of a centralized Internship Management System (IMS) that strengthens communication between 
students, employers, and institutions. While their study explains the technical design very well, it 
pays less attention to the user experience or how such systems actually help students grow and 
prepare for jobs.  Enhancing Employability Through Work Integrated Learning article discusses 
internships from an educational point of view, showing how Work -Integrated Learning (WIL) 
helps students become more career -ready. However, the study does not explore how technology 
or digital systems can support these learning experiences. It points out challenges in connecting 
universities and industries but does not offer digital solutions to fix them. 
Empirical Evaluation of Popular Proctoring Tools gives a detailed comparison of popular online 
proctoring systems and how they work to prevent cheating during online exams. However, the 
study mainly looks at advanced and strict proctoring tools that rely on cameras, AI, and lockdown 
software. It does not cons ider lighter or less intrusive methods that could protect exam integrity 
while reducing privacy concerns.  Analysis of self-directed learning (SDL) showed that formative 
quizzes can help improve student s’ performance by supporting self -directed learning, but some 
gaps remain. The study only focused on first-year medical students from one course, so the results 
may not apply to other subjects or groups. It also used only numerical data and did not explore  
students’ opinions or experiences.  Assessing Education Students’ Self -Directed Learning offers 
valuable insights into the self -directed learning abilities and technology readiness of education 
students engaged in online learning.  However, the study mainly describes learners’ attitudes and 
preferences without showing how these can be directly applied to improve online course design. 
It does not test whether specific types of learning materials or quizzes actually enhance self -
directed learning.

4. System Review 
4.1 InternSathi 
 
InternSathi is a Nepal-based internship platform that positions itself as a bridge between freshers 
and companies looking for interns or junior staff. Being founded in around 2021 their website 
indicates that it has helped over 2,500 freshers secure internships, works with more than 435 
companies, and is connected to more than 20 training institutes. 
For candidates, InternSathi allows profile creation, CV uploads, browsing of internship (and 
entry-level job) listings, and applying directly via the portal. For employers, it offers a way to post 
internships for free and access a pool of registered candid ates. The platform also claims to offer 
cost-effective solutions for companies, brand visibility among the young talent pool, and training 
partner integrations. The primary strength of InternSathi is that it is a local -oriented company, 
which is tailored t o the Nepali market. This will enable it to be more knowledgeable about the 
local needs, establish contacts with the local institutions, and provide services to the students and 
companies more efficiently. Its free posting option and the broad variety of i nternship type make 
it a convenient site to both the job seekers and the employers. 
 
4.2 Intern Nepal 
Intern Nepal is another Nepal-based site that is aimed at providing internship and entry level job 
placements in Nepal. It was established in 2022, the purpose of which is to match young candidates 
with internship opportunities and assist organizations in finding new talents. 
The site enables candidates to create accounts, post their resumes and apply to jobs or internship 
opportunities on advertised positions. Conversely, employers are able to sign up to post 
opportunities and hire interns. Besides the placement facilitation, Intern Nepal has short 
professional courses and workshops that aim at building job -specific skills prior to the internship 
or during the internship. This aspect of training contributes to the difference between it and other 
platforms and assists in bridging the gap of skills which is usually evident among fresh graduates. 
Intern Nepal is also a holistic career development platform not a matching service by combining 
internship listing and skill development.

4.3 NepalDev  
NepalDev is a user-friendly internship application  that targets fresh graduates and young job 
hunters in Nepal. Its well -structured and clean interface has a simple and well -structured 
navigation bar that makes the site easily intuitive to the first time user. The site boasts of useful 
functionality, inclu ding job searches, internship programs, creating a profile, and accessing a 
variety of career resources. It also invites people to search jobs or create a professional profile 
within a few seconds. The resources section of NepalDev seems to be the most pro mising part of 
the platform, with the help of professional design, favourable statistics, and regular security 
protocols, and released policies.  
However, the platform is not as functional in practice as smooth as it may appear. Users cannot 
create an account, log in or even view the key functions it boasts of such as the resources section 
itself. This brings about issues of the operational preparedness of  the platform and its general 
reliability to the job seekers.  
 
4.4 Internshala 
Internshala is one of the major Indian job -tech platforms that can be used to match students and 
fresh graduates with entry -level job and internship opportunities. It has more than 300,000 
companies and 21 million candidates, having been founded in 2010 by Sarvesh Agrawal. Besides 
listing internships, the site also provides on -line training opportunities and certification courses 
which help in improving the employability skills in different fields. 
The site enables students to make profiles, construct resumes and apply to internships with the 
help of filters like location, duration, stipend and work mode. A centralized dashboard allows 
students to monitor applications, employers to post internships, manage applicants, and get 
premium services to increase visibility. The fact that Internshala combines both the skill 
development and the recruitment process, i.e. it provides certification courses, placement courses 
with AI and online degrees is what makes it unlike the traditional job boards and makes it widely 
used.

4.5 InternDekho 
InternDekho refers itself as a mechanism between a group of aspiring interns and employers in 
different fields in India. InternDekho focuses on the ease of use, wide range of internship 
opportunities, and a corresponding algorithm that matches the candidat es with the opportunities, 
depending on their preferences and skills. 
The strong points of InternDekho are its unambiguous statements of matching technology and 
quality assurance: it emphasizes on the strong matching algorithm, quality assurance of employers, 
and real time notifications to keep the users informed. The design is easy to use and intuitive which 
is advantageous considering the fact that the design is focusing on younger students or freshers 
who might not be very conversant with the internship search platform. In addition, the emphasis 
on the diversification of o pportunities in the industries enables it to cover more ground and 
therefore non-technical students can also be useful in the platform.

5. System Review Gap Analysis 
Platforms such as Internshala and InternDekho which operate in India are much more functional 
and user-friendly than the current internship websites in Nepal. They have well-developed systems 
with interesting features that provide an extensive variety of s ervices such as internship 
opportunities, training, and career support. But this richness of features can at times overwhelm 
the interface making it look crowded and the user may experience being overwhelmed or be unable 
to navigate the interface effectively. 
However, Nepal-based websites like InternSathi or Intern Nepal have evident functionality and 
usability deficiencies. An example is that, where Internshala only needs users to fill in their profile 
and application form one time, and thus the user can easil y apply to several internships with the 
same saved CV, the InternSathi application process is still long and repetitive and can be 
discouraging. Moreover, in the case of Intern Nepal, users are allowed to enroll into paid training 
programs without proper verification or payment confirmation, which is a security and reliability 
concern. The majority of local platforms also lack a resource or training section to assist the 
students in advancing their skills, hence, it is less effective in terms of career growth on the whole. 
Such findings reveal how a more organized, easy-to-use, and open internship platform in Nepal is 
necessary, one that does not only simplify the application process but also incorporates verified 
training materials and improve the general user confidence and interest.

6. Implications for InternQuest 
The review of studies and system analyses has shown that there is a need for an integrated, web -
based platform that links students, employers, and institutions of learning effectively. Researchers 
such as  (Shriwas, et al., 2024)  and (Mydyti & Kadriu, 2020)  have indicated how web -based 
systems ease internship coordination by promoting transparency. Further, the review of the 
existing system indicates that existing Nepali platforms like InternSathi, NepalDev, and Intern 
Nepal either have weak verification mech anisms, are outdated, or provide weak mechanisms for 
tracking. Based on the findings, InternQuest should focus on developing an integrated, reliable 
system where students will find verified opportunities, apply with ease, and track their progress in 
real time. 
(Kenaphoom & Niyomves, 2023)  also illustrate that work -integrated learning raises students' 
preparedness for work. Thus, InternQuest should not be limited to a mere listing of opportunities 
but should offer features to students in their preparation for internships, such as study resou rces, 
quizzes for self-assessment, and progress tracking. These tools will help users highlight skill gaps 
and thus be more confident and capable when applying, aligning the platform with both academic 
and professional goals. 
Research related to online assessment and proctoring indicates that there are challenges related to 
privacy, accessibility, and user comfort . (Oh & Hyunyong, 2022) , (Hussein, et al., 2020)  These 
findings suggest that InternQuest can incorporate lightweight assessment features like timed 
quizzes or tab -focus tracking instead of using intrusive AI systems. InternQuest attempts to 
implement or combine lessons learned from both academic research and this review of the system 
in its quest to be a trusted, user -friendly platform that is supportive of education as it bridges the 
students with verified employers and address the weaknesses in existing systems.
